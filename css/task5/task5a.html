Floating Articles

Cats are curious creatures. They love to explore and play, often getting into small spaces and climbing to high spots.
Domestic cats enjoy napping in sunny spots and purring when happy. They also have unique personalities and can be affectionate companions.
Some cats are very vocal, meowing to communicate with their owners. Others prefer silent companionship, showing love through head bumps and purring.
How do I love my cats? Let me count the ways. The clean snap of three-year-old Larry’s jaw as he contemplates me with detached curiosity is my favourite sound in the world. I love the tenor and cadence of my six-month-old kitten Kedi’s miaows as he follows me around the house. (High-pitched indignant squeaks means he wants food; lower-pitched chirrups suggest he would like to play.) I love the weight of Larry on my feet at night and the scratchy caress of Kedi’s tongue on my eyelid in the morning. But how do I know what these little tykes really think and feel? I fear the authors of online listicles written in cursive fonts are unlikely to provide me with the latest scientific research – and are probably just saying what they think I want to hear. To truly journey into the feline soul, I will have to go to the fountainhead.
Cats are natural hunters. They enjoy stalking and chasing toys, which helps them stay active and mentally stimulated.
Grooming is an important habit. Cats spend hours cleaning themselves to maintain a sleek coat and stay healthy.
Playtime strengthens the bond with humans. Interactive toys and games keep cats engaged and happy.
